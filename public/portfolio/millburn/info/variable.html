About MHS:



Millburn High School is an outstanding secondary institution that offers students a rigorous, stimulating, and diverse academic program delivered by an experienced and dedicated faculty. Our students perform at an extraordinarily high level while maintaining substantial involvement in interscholastic sports, clubs and activities and community service. For more than sixteen years, Millburn High School has been consistently recognized as one of the 'best' high schools in New Jersey by such publications as New Jersey Magazine and Money Magazine. Recently, Millburn High School was ranked the number one high school in the United States by Jay Matthews, Education Writer for the Washington Post, in his book Class Struggle: What's Wrong (And Right) With America's Best Public High Schools.

The program of studies at Millburn High School offers 140 courses including 18 Advanced Placement courses, a challenging honors program, specialized electives, and unique programs in Special Education, Independent Study, Unified Studies, Marketing Education, Life Skills and Youth Community Service. The performance of our students in this curriculum has been exceptional.

We are extremely proud of the programs offered by our Special Services Department. Instruction in our Resource Room, ESL and BSI classes is exemplary. The implementation of in-class support and inclusion strategies has enabled us to provide individualized instruction in many courses. Currently, curriculum writing in all academic disciplines is being reviewed to include core curriculum standards.

Millburn High School students are also extensively involved in extracurricular activities. Approximately 50% of the student body participates each season in an athletic program that includes 26 varsity sports. Bands, orchestras, choruses and specialty vocal and instrumental groups boast membership of 325. In addition, students have the opportunity to develop leadership potential in such endeavors as student publications, dramatic productions, political groups and classroom related clubs. Volunteerism is emphasized through school-wide service organizations, a peer tutoring program and community related projects.
